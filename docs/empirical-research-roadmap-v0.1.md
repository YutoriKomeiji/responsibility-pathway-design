# RPD Empirical Research Roadmap v0.1

## Purpose

This roadmap translates the empirical validation and falsification protocols into a staged research program. It does not claim that the stages have been completed.

## Stage 0 — Protocol stabilization

Objectives:

- review operational definitions;
- test whether independent reviewers can apply the protocols;
- identify ambiguous metrics and overlapping propositions;
- record likely ethical, legal, privacy, security, and access constraints.

Exit evidence:

- at least two independent protocol walkthroughs;
- documented disagreements and revisions;
- a bounded initial case-selection plan.

## Stage 1 — Retrospective reconstruction

Objectives:

- reconstruct multiple incidents or near misses;
- compare RPD diagnosis with existing incident analyses;
- identify whether RPD adds distinct pathway findings;
- test whether proposed interventions remain traceable to evidence.

Minimum diversity targets:

- different organizational structures;
- different levels of reversibility;
- at least one cross-organizational case;
- at least one case with unresolved residual impact;
- at least one negative or null case.

No operational-effectiveness claim is available at this stage.

## Stage 2 — Prospective design comparison

Objectives:

- compare RPD-informed designs with credible baselines and alternatives;
- measure traceability, conflict visibility, response-option coverage, reviewer agreement, and burden;
- identify cases where a simpler method performs equally or better.

Required controls:

- comparable time and expertise across conditions;
- predeclared outcomes;
- independent review where feasible;
- explicit treatment of normative disagreement.

## Stage 3 — Simulation and tabletop exercise

Objectives:

- test whether authorities and interventions can be exercised;
- measure latency, evidence access, handoff integrity, recovery ownership, and reopening;
- test actor absence, overload, ambiguity, and cross-boundary failure.

Required scenarios include:

- primary owner unavailable;
- stop authority delayed or contested;
- evidence incomplete or contradictory;
- temporary delegation expires;
- rollback succeeds but recovery remains incomplete;
- affected-party challenge arrives after nominal closure;
- supplier or institutional boundary changes;
- multiple patterns conflict under time pressure.

## Stage 4 — Bounded field pilot

Objectives:

- observe practical use in a limited real workflow;
- compare with baseline operation where feasible;
- measure benefits, burdens, workarounds, exclusion, and unexpected effects;
- exercise stop, fallback, challenge, and withdrawal routes.

Entry conditions:

- explicit human authorization;
- predeclared stopping conditions;
- monitoring and evidence-preservation plan;
- appropriate institutional, ethical, legal, privacy, security, and safety review;
- credible fallback that does not depend on the experimental design.

## Stage 5 — Longitudinal and cross-case study

Objectives:

- determine whether pathway properties persist through organizational and technical change;
- compare transfer conditions across sectors and institutions;
- identify context-specific variants;
- assess whether RPD's incremental value persists after novelty and intensive support decline.

Important outcomes:

- claim survival and downgrade rates;
- pattern revision, suspension, and retirement;
- reopening frequency and quality;
- residual-impact stewardship over time;
- sustained burden and resource requirements.

## Stage 6 — External replication and critique

Objectives:

- enable independent teams to apply and challenge the method;
- compare interpretations and results;
- test for author or reviewer allegiance effects;
- surface terminology, scope, and power-distribution problems.

Repository support should eventually include:

- stable versioned protocols;
- de-identified reference cases where lawful and ethical;
- scoring or coding guidance only where empirically justified;
- negative and null-result records;
- status labels for active, experimental, suspended, and retired objects;
- migration guidance between versions.

## Cross-stage gates

Progression to a later stage does not erase limitations from earlier stages.

At every stage, ask:

1. Is the claim narrower than the evidence?
2. Is the comparator credible?
3. Are burdens and power effects visible?
4. Are affected parties represented appropriately?
5. Could the result be explained without RPD?
6. What evidence would weaken the conclusion?
7. Should an object be retained, revised, restricted, suspended, or retired?
8. Has a human authorized the next level of exposure or operation?

## Priority study sequence

A practical initial sequence is:

1. one retrospective ERP workflow case;
2. one cross-organizational delegation case;
3. one contestability and remedy case;
4. one high-irreversibility case;
5. one low-stakes case testing burden disproportionality;
6. one explicit null or negative comparison.

The sequence is deliberately mixed. Selecting only cases likely to favor RPD would weaken the research program.

## Research boundary

This roadmap describes work required to establish, narrow, revise, or reject empirical propositions. It is not evidence that RPD is validated, superior, safe, lawful, fair, compliant, production-ready, or socially accepted.
